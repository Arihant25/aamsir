Employee Leave Policy — Acme University

1. Overview
This policy outlines the types of leave available to all full-time employees of Acme University, the procedures for requesting leave, and the responsibilities of both employees and supervisors.

2. Types of Leave

2.1 Annual Leave (Vacation)
All full-time employees are entitled to 20 working days of paid annual leave per calendar year. Leave accrues at a rate of 1.67 days per month. Unused leave may be carried forward up to a maximum of 10 days into the next calendar year. Any leave beyond 10 days that is not used by March 31 of the following year will be forfeited.

2.2 Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days. Sick leave does not accrue and unused days do not carry forward.

2.3 Personal Leave
Up to 5 days of personal leave may be taken per year for personal matters including family emergencies, moving, or other urgent personal business. Personal leave requires supervisor approval at least 24 hours in advance when possible.

2.4 Parental Leave
Primary caregivers are entitled to 16 weeks of paid parental leave. Secondary caregivers are entitled to 4 weeks of paid parental leave. Parental leave must commence within 12 months of the birth or adoption of a child.

2.5 Bereavement Leave
Employees may take up to 5 days of paid bereavement leave for the death of an immediate family member (spouse, parent, child, sibling). Up to 3 days may be taken for extended family members.

3. Leave Request Procedure
All leave requests must be submitted through the HR Portal at least 5 working days in advance for planned leave. Requests should include the type of leave, dates, and any supporting documentation. Supervisors must respond to leave requests within 2 working days.

4. Public Holidays
Acme University observes 11 public holidays per year as gazetted by the government. When a public holiday falls on a weekend, the following Monday will be observed as a substitute holiday.

5. Leave Without Pay
In exceptional circumstances, employees may request unpaid leave for up to 30 days per year. Such requests require approval from the department head and HR director. Unpaid leave exceeding 30 days may affect benefits and seniority calculations.
