Standard Operating Procedure: Travel Reimbursement

SOP Number: FIN-2024-003
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Department: Finance & Accounts
Approved By: CFO, Acme University

1. Purpose
This SOP establishes the procedures for submitting and processing travel expense reimbursements for all university employees who travel on official business.

2. Scope
This procedure applies to all employees, faculty, and authorized contractors who incur travel expenses while conducting university business. This includes domestic and international travel.

3. Pre-Travel Requirements
3.1 All travel must be pre-approved by the employee's department head using Form TR-01 (Travel Authorization).
3.2 International travel requires additional approval from the Dean/VP level.
3.3 Travel bookings should be made through the university's preferred travel agency (GlobalTravel Corp.) to obtain negotiated rates.

4. Eligible Expenses
4.1 Transportation: Economy class airfare, train tickets, rental cars (compact/mid-size only), taxi/rideshare, mileage at $0.655/mile for personal vehicle use.
4.2 Accommodation: Up to $200/night for domestic travel, up to $350/night for international travel. Receipts required.
4.3 Meals: Per diem of $75/day for domestic travel, $100/day for international travel. Alternatively, actual expenses up to these limits with receipts.
4.4 Conference/Registration Fees: Full reimbursement with receipt and proof of attendance.
4.5 Miscellaneous: Internet charges, parking fees, baggage fees — with receipts.

5. Submission Process
5.1 Complete Form TR-02 (Expense Reimbursement) within 30 days of travel completion.
5.2 Attach all original receipts (or clear digital scans).
5.3 Submit through the Finance Portal under "Travel Claims."
5.4 Department head must approve the claim within 5 business days.
5.5 Finance will process approved claims within 10 business days.

6. Non-Reimbursable Expenses
- Alcoholic beverages
- Entertainment or personal excursions
- First/business class upgrades (unless pre-approved for flights over 8 hours)
- Travel insurance (university provides blanket coverage)
- Fines, penalties, or traffic violations
- Expenses for accompanying family members

7. Late Submissions
Claims submitted more than 60 days after travel completion may be denied. Exceptions require written justification from the department head and approval from the Finance Director.
