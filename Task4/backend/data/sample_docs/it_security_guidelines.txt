IT Security Guidelines — Acme University

Document ID: IT-SEC-2024-001
Classification: Internal Use Only
Last Updated: September 2024

1. Purpose
These guidelines establish the information security standards for all university IT systems, networks, and data. Compliance is mandatory for all employees, faculty, students, and contractors who access university IT resources.

2. Password Policy
2.1 Minimum length: 12 characters
2.2 Must include: uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and at least one special character
2.3 Passwords must be changed every 90 days
2.4 Previous 12 passwords cannot be reused
2.5 Account lockout: 5 failed attempts triggers a 30-minute lockout
2.6 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is required for all staff accounts and VPN access

3. Data Classification
3.1 Public: Information intended for public release (course catalogs, press releases)
3.2 Internal: General internal information (meeting notes, internal memos)
3.3 Confidential: Sensitive information (student records, financial data, HR records)
3.4 Restricted: Highly sensitive (research data under NDA, medical records, SSNs)

4. Acceptable Use
4.1 University IT resources are provided for academic and administrative purposes
4.2 Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work duties
4.3 Prohibited activities include: unauthorized access attempts, cryptocurrency mining, hosting unauthorized services, distribution of copyrighted material, harassment or illegal activities

5. Email Security
5.1 Do not open attachments from unknown senders
5.2 Report suspected phishing emails to security@acme.edu
5.3 Confidential information must not be sent via unencrypted email
5.4 Use the approved email encryption tool for sensitive communications

6. Network Security
6.1 All devices connecting to the university network must have current antivirus software
6.2 Personal devices must be registered with IT before connecting to the corporate network
6.3 Guest WiFi (AcmeGuest) is available for visitors — no access to internal resources
6.4 VPN must be used for remote access to internal systems

7. Incident Response
7.1 Report all security incidents immediately to the IT Security team (ext. 4500 or security@acme.edu)
7.2 Do not attempt to investigate or remediate on your own
7.3 Preserve any evidence (screenshots, logs, suspicious emails)
7.4 The IT Security team will respond within 1 hour during business hours, 4 hours after hours

8. Physical Security
8.1 Lock your workstation when leaving your desk (Win+L or Ctrl+Cmd+Q)
8.2 Do not leave sensitive documents on your desk overnight (clean desk policy)
8.3 Report lost or stolen devices immediately to IT Security
8.4 Server rooms and network closets require badge access — no tailgating

9. Compliance
Violation of these guidelines may result in disciplinary action, including suspension of IT access, formal reprimand, or termination. Criminal violations will be reported to law enforcement.
