Research Funding and Grants Policy — Acme University

Policy Number: RES-2024-001
Effective Date: August 1, 2024
Office of Research and Sponsored Programs (ORSP)

1. Purpose
This policy governs the application, management, and reporting of research funding for all faculty and approved graduate students at Acme University.

2. Types of Funding

2.1 Internal Seed Grants
- Available to all tenure-track and tenured faculty
- Maximum amount: $25,000 per project
- Duration: 12 months
- Application deadline: Rolling (quarterly review)
- Purpose: Pilot studies, preliminary data collection for external grant applications

2.2 Faculty Development Grants
- Available to all faculty members
- Maximum amount: $10,000 per year
- Covers: Conference travel, workshop attendance, publication fees, equipment
- Requires department chair endorsement

2.3 Graduate Research Assistantships
- Available to full-time graduate students in good academic standing
- Stipend: $2,500/month for Masters, $3,000/month for PhD students
- Includes tuition waiver
- Renewable annually based on satisfactory progress

3. External Grant Applications

3.1 All external grant applications must be routed through the ORSP at least 15 business days before the sponsor deadline.
3.2 The university requires a minimum 10% cost-sharing on federal grants unless waived by the Provost.
3.3 Indirect cost rate: 52% of modified total direct costs (MTDC) for on-campus research, 26% for off-campus.
3.4 PI eligibility: Must be full-time faculty. Co-PI may include research staff and adjunct faculty.

4. Budget Management

4.1 All grant expenditures must comply with the sponsor's terms and university procurement policies.
4.2 Budget modifications exceeding 10% of a line item require ORSP approval.
4.3 No-cost extensions must be requested at least 30 days before the project end date.
4.4 Unspent funds must be returned to the sponsor unless a no-cost extension is approved.

5. Intellectual Property

5.1 Inventions and discoveries made using university resources or funding are jointly owned by the university and the inventor(s).
5.2 The Technology Transfer Office (TTO) handles patent applications and licensing.
5.3 Revenue from licensed IP is split: 40% inventor(s), 30% department, 30% university.

6. Compliance and Ethics

6.1 All research involving human subjects requires IRB approval before data collection begins.
6.2 Animal research requires IACUC approval.
6.3 Research misconduct (fabrication, falsification, plagiarism) will be investigated per federal guidelines and may result in termination and debarment.
6.4 Conflict of interest disclosures are required annually and for each new grant application.

7. Reporting

7.1 Interim reports: Required every 6 months for internal grants, per sponsor schedule for external grants.
7.2 Final reports: Due within 90 days of project completion.
7.3 Publications resulting from funded research must acknowledge the funding source.
